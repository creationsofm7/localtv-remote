A free, open-source Unified Remote alternative for Windows

If you came here looking for a free Unified Remote alternative, here's the honest version: LocalTV Remote does one thing — turns your phone into a mouse, keyboard and volume remote for a Windows PC — and it does it with no app on your phone, no account, and no cloud. Below is a side-by-side comparison, including the cases where Unified Remote is the better choice.

When LocalTV Remote is the better pick

You're on Windows, you want a truly free and open-source tool, and you don't want to install anything on the phones doing the controlling — friends and family just scan a QR code and get their own remote. You care that input stays on your own network, with nothing routed through a cloud or tied to an account.

When Unified Remote is the better pick

You need to control a Mac or Linux machine, you want Bluetooth as well as Wi-Fi, or you rely on its deep library of app-specific remotes, media controls and custom macros. Unified Remote is older and broader; LocalTV Remote is deliberately narrow and Windows-only. If breadth matters more than openness, it's the safer bet.
